Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is a flavorful, one-pot rice dish commonly enjoyed in Nigeria and many parts of West Africa. It's made with tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices.

Ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity Price
Rice 2-3 cups of parboiled rice 2500 naira
Tomatoes 4 large tomatoes or 1 can of tomato paste 500 naira
Onion 1 large onion 100 naira
Bell pepper 1 large bell pepper 200 naira
Vegetable oil 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil 200 naira
Thyme, Curry powder, ginger-garlic paste, seasoning cubes 1 teaspoon each 1000 naira
Salt to taste As preferred 50 naira

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly until the water runs clear. Set aside.
  2. In a blender, combine the tomatoes, red bell pepper, and half of the onions. Blend into a smooth paste.
  3. Heat oil in a large pot. Add the remaining chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic paste (if using) and cook for another minute.
  4. Add the blended tomato paste to the pot. Stir well and cook for 10-15 minutes until the oil begins to separate from the tomato sauce.
  5. Stir in the thyme, curry powder, seasoning cubes, and sugar (if using). Let it simmer for a few more minutes.
  6. Pour in the rinsed rice and mix well to coat the rice with the tomato sauce.
  7. Once the rice is cooked and all the liquid is absorbed, fluff the rice and serve. You can garnish with roasted chicken or fried plantains.

Egusi Soup

Egusi soup is one of Nigeria's most famous soups, made from melon seeds (egusi), vegetables, and meat or fish. It’s usually served with pounded yam, fufu, or semovita.

Ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity Price
Ground egusi (melon seeds) 2 cups 600 naira
Assorted meats and stockfish (beef, goat meat, shaki, etc) 1 Kg 5000 naira
Onion, crayfish, and pepper 1 large onion (chopped), 1 tablespoon of crayfish, and grounded pepper 200 naira
Palm oil 3 tablespoons 200 naira
Vegetable (pumpkin, bitterleaf as preferred) Half kg 200 naira
Seasoning cubes and salt to taste As preferred 100 naira

Cooking Instructions:

  • In a pot, add the assorted meats, onion, seasoning cubes, salt, and some water. Cook until the meats are tender (about 45 minutes). Add the dried fish or stockfish towards the end of cooking.
  • In a separate bowl, mix the ground egusi with a bit of water to form a smooth paste.
  • In a large pot, heat the palm oil until it melts and turns slightly red. Add the chopped onions and sauté for about 2-3 minutes. Add the blended red bell pepper, scotch bonnet peppers (if using), and a pinch of salt, and cook for another 5-7 minutes.
  • Pour in the meat and fish, along with the broth from cooking the meats. Stir everything together and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  • Add the chopped vegetables and ground crayfish, and cook for another 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend together.
  • Taste the soup, and adjust with more seasoning cubes, salt, or pepper as needed. Let it simmer for a few more minutes.
  • Serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or any other preferred side.

Suya

Suya (Grilled Meat Skewers) is a popular Nigerian street food made of skewered and grilled spiced meat, often served with onions, tomatoes, and spicy pepper sauce.

Ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity Price
Beef 1-2 kg 6000 naira
Ground peanuts, paprika, thyme 2 tablespoons each 1000 naira
Ground ginger, garlic powder, cayenne pepper, onion powder 1 tablespoon each 1500 naira
Vegetable oil 1-2 tablespoons 200 naira
Skewers (wooden or metal) 5-10 500 naira
Seasoning cubes powder As preferred 100 naira

Cooking Instructions:

  • Cut the beef into thin strips. In a large bowl, combine the paprika, cayenne pepper, ginger, garlic powder, thyme, onion powder, ground peanuts, salt, and ground pepper. Add a little vegetable oil to make a marinade paste.
  • Coat the beef strips in the spice mix. Make sure each piece is well-coated. Cover and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour (or overnight in the fridge for better flavor).
  • If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for about 20 minutes to prevent burning. Thread the marinated beef onto the skewers.
  • Preheat a grill or barbecue to medium-high heat. Grill the meat on each side for about 3-4 minutes or until it’s cooked to your liking.
